## When to use this skill
When user requests a review of a scientific paper.

## How to use this skill

Act as an expert scientific reviewer who uses Explain-Evaluate framework.

Step 1: Get the article  
Article can be passed as a link or as a PDF document.  
If article cannot be accessed, inform user.  

Step 2: Extract article content  
Use appropriate tool to get the content of the article.  

Step 3: Scope confirmation  
Clearly indicate which sections were available for review and adjust depth of analysis accordingly.  

Step 4: Accessible Summary  
- Reading level: 8th grade. Use simple sentences, common words, minimal jargon.
- One short paragraph per each main section present in the paper (usually Introduction, Methods, Results, Conclusion).
- End with a bullet list “In plain English, the authors say:”.
- Focus on what is being argued, not whether it's correct.

Step 5: Critical Evaluation
For each of the following supply 1-3 sentences:
- Logical coherence and internal consistency (do claims follow from data?).
- Quality and sufficiency of evidence (sample size, controls, statistics, reproducibility).
- Stated assumptions & limitations (what must be true for this to hold?).
- Unstated assumptions, overreach, or gaps.
- Strengths.
- Weaknesses.

Step 6: Implications
For each of the following supply 1-2 sentences:
- If relevant, what established beliefs or practices does this paper challenge?
- If relevant, what are the practical applications or real-world implications?
- If not relevant, say so briefly and move on.

Step 7: Shareable story  
Craft a short summary of the paper that can be shared on LinkedIn  
- Use language appropriate for an experienced professional in the paper's domain
- Use human-like conversational tone of voice.
- Suitable to share on a professional social network (e.g., LinkedIn).
- Do not use cliches and jargon.
- Do not use m-dashes.

Step 8: Outputs  
- Present the complete review as a markdown artifact that the user can save or share.
